## Introduction

The data availability statement is part of the back matter of a manuscript. The back matter usually also contains information on conflicts of interest, author information, acknowledgements and the references. 

The data availability statement enables linking of the manuscript (via DOIs, alternatively using the repository-specific identifiers or accession numbers) to the corresponding FAIR dataset in research data repositories. 

## Templates for Data Availability Statements

<b>Data in a publicly accessible repository with DOI</b>

The data generated and analysed during this study are openly available in [repository name] at [DOI as URL].

<b>Data available in a public repository with DOI following an embargo from the date of publication</b>

The data generated and analysed during this study will be openly available in [repository name] at [DOI as URL] following an embargo from the date of publication.

<b>Data derived from public domain resources</b>

The data used for/in  this study are openly available in [repository name] at [DOI as URL]. The data were derived from sources available in the public domain [list of resources]. 

<b>Dataset owned by a third party and restrictions apply to availability</b>

The data analysed in this study were provided by [third party] under licence/by permission and are not publicly available. Data are available from the corresponding author upon request and with permission of [third party, including contact information].

<b>Data available on request to the corresponding author due to restrictions by ethical reasons and legal or privacy regulations</b>

The data generated and analysed in this study are not openly available due to [reasons, including explanation]. The data are available from the corresponding author on reasonable request [mention requirements for access].

<b>Data sharing not applicable, as no new data is generated</b>

Data sharing is not applicable, as no new data was generated or analysed in this study.
