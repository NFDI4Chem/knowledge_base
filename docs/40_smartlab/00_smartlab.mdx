# Smart Laboratory (SmartLab)

![smartlab_flow](/img/smartlab/smartlab_flow2.png)

**SmartLab – a holistic, FAIR‑centric approach to research data management in chemistry**  

A **SmartLab** integrates every step of a chemist’s workflow — from planning to archiving — into a seamless digital ecosystem. Unlike a traditional Laboratory Management System (LIMS), the primary goal of a SmartLab is to realise the [**FAIR data principles**](/docs/fair) (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Re‑usable).

1. **Planning & documentation** – The researcher records the experimental design in an [**electronic lab notebook (ELN)**](/docs/eln).  
2. **Data acquisition** – Instruments (e.g., spectrometers) push raw data directly into the ELN via an [**Application Programming Interface (API)**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/API).  
3. **Automatic metadata & format conversion** – The ELN assigns the appropriate [**metadata**](/docs/metadata) and converts proprietary data formats into open, [**standards‑compliant formats**](/docs/format_standards).  
4. **Structured, machine‑readable records** – Both the (meta)data and the experimental description are organised in a way that is readable by humans and machines, e.g., using [**machine‑readable chemical structures**](/docs/machine-readable_chemical_structures).  
5. **Publishing & archiving** – When the researcher decides to publish or archive the work, the ELN already supplies a FAIR‑ready package that can be ingested by a [**repository**](/docs/repositories) or **archive** with minimal additional effort.

In short, a SmartLab turns the entire [research data lifecycle](/docs/data_life_cycle) into a coherent, automated process, freeing scientists to focus on discovery rather than data wrangling.
